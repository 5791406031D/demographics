DEMOGRAPHY OF GENOCIDE

Case study: Multipolar Orange County demographics

The population of Orange county has increased ___ since the 1980s. The population of every demographic group has risen, but the growth rate of whites has slowed dramatically. This development occurred in tandem with explosive increases in non-white races and ethnicities.

Importantly, the growth of the non-white population has not been dispersed throughout the county. Rather, non-white populations have grown in particular geographic localities in central county. Hispanics, an ethnicity, increased mainly in Santa Ana-Anaheim corridor. Asians, a race, increased mainly in the Westminster-Garden Grove corridor. The five cities of highest growth from 1980 to 2010 census were Anaheim, La Habra, Santa Ana, Tustin, and Stanton.

×

These epicenters of non-white populations have displaced the white residents in these cities. By 2010, over half of whites had disappeared from Garden Grove, Stanton, Westminster, Anaheim, and Buena Park. The white demographic either migrated or retreated into the coastline, south county, and north-east county.

The result of these developments is racial segregation and cultural tension. Orange County has multipolar epicenters of racial populations and where cultures dominate interactions, by language especially and how businesses are marketed to which groups.

The cities which are the most "diverse" by race and citizenhood tend to be the cities with the least amount of wealth, highest unemployment rates.

Forecast

The Hispanic pole

The Hispanic pole has already begun and will continue to encompass the western periphery of Tustin. Tustin's southern periphery is also threatened by the surging Asian population in Irvine.

The Asian pole

A separate and surging pole is the growing dominance in Irvine, the heart of Orange county. In terms of expansion from this node, Asians cannot extend north-east, east, south-east due to unincorporated or non-residential zoned land, and west due to the airport; they can extend north into Tustin's east side, leaving the whites as a buffer to the Hispanics on the west side. The rich Asians can also slowly penetrate the white bastion of Newport Beach, but in thirty years the city experienced only a 7.5 percent increase in the Asian population.

The White pole

The cities which remained the most White throughout the examined thirty year period are Newport Beach, Dana Point, San Clemente, Mission Viejo, and Laguna. Newport Beach led the pack in every census, having ceded only ~13% of its White population in thirty years compared to the average ___%. This city is also the most wealthy of the county, with a per capita income nearly $30,000 greater than its runner-up, Dana Point. In fact, the rank of Orange county cities by per capita income reads almost in parallel with the ranking of cities which are most White.

The White strongholds during the course of the examined 30 year period are Newport Beach, Dana Point, San Clemente, Mission Viejo, and Laguna areas. All of these strongholds voted Trump in the 2016 election. Whereas the only city below a 80 percent white population in 1980 was Santa Ana, today Newport Beach is the only city which still has a white population above 80 percent.

Given the downward trend for Whites throughout the country, there is little reason to doubt their decline will accelerate. The geographic pockets of whites are being split and are eroding at the peripheries.

The most endangered areas appear to be Costa Mesa, which represents a split of whites in Huntington and Newport Beaches; Tustin, to the south and the west; Huntington Beach, to the north and east. Irvine is now safely in the Asian column.
